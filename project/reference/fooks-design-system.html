Introduction

The Fooks Design System features a thoughtfully curated set of color styles and typography, providing an ideal foundation for their brand and projects. Understanding the importance of color contrast, we've integrated WCAG 2.1 contrast ratio guidelines directly into our color system. This ensures that all designs meet accessibility standards, enhancing legibility and inclusivity across their work.

Typography

The Fooks Design System includes a well-structured typography system designed to reflect their brand's identity and ensure consistency across all projects. Each type style is carefully crafted for readability and hierarchy, providing clear communication in every context.

Colors

The Fooks Design System features a thoughtfully curated set of color styles, providing an ideal foundation for their brand and projects. Understanding the importance of color contrast, we've integrated WCAG 2.1 contrast ratio guidelines directly into our color system.

Primary colors

These are the main colors that make up the majority of the colors used in the design system.

Base

Base black and white color styles to quickly swap out if you need to.

Gray

Gray is a neutral color and is the foundation of the color system. Almost everything in UI design — text, form fields, backgrounds, dividers — are usually gray.

Brand

The brand color is your "primary" color, and is used across all interactive elements such as buttons, links, inputs, etc. This color can define the overall feel and can elicit emotion.

Error

Error colors are used across error states and in "destructive" actions. They communicate a destructive/negative action, such as removing a user from your team.

Warning

Warning colors can communicate that an action is potentially destructive or "on-hold". These colors are commonly used in confirmations to grab the users' attention.

Success

Success colors communicate a positive action, positive trend, or a successful confirmation. If you're using green as your primary color, it can be helpful to introduce a different hue for your success green.

Secondary colors

Along with primary colors, it's helpful to have a selection of secondary colors to use in components such as pills, alerts and labels.

Layout
